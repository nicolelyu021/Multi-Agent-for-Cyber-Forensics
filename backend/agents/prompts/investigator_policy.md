# Corporate Rules and Code of Ethics

*Source: Corporate Code of Ethics, Department of Justice Archive*

## Core Principle
Employees must conduct business in accordance with all applicable laws and in a moral, honest, and ethical manner. Compliance is a condition of employment. Violations may result in disciplinary action, including termination.

## Rule Set (Graph, Traffic, and Role Analysis)

**R3 — Ethical Business Conduct**
Employees must act in the best interests of the company and must not engage in behavior detrimental to the company or for personal gain derived from their role.
*Agent signals:*
- Personal gain tied to company decisions
- Behavior that benefits the individual at company expense
- Actions that harm company reputation

**R5 — Contract and Legal Process Integrity**
All agreements must be properly reviewed and approved, especially by legal where required.
*Agent signals:*
- Attempts to bypass legal review
- Informal agreements creating obligations without approval
- Pressure to finalize agreements outside process

**R6 — Confidential Information Protection**
Company confidential and proprietary information must not be disclosed or used improperly.
*Agent signals:*
- Sharing sensitive internal data
- Use of non-public information for advantage
- Discussions of confidential data in inappropriate contexts

**R9 — Proper Use of Communication Systems**
Company communication systems (email, phone, data systems) must be used responsibly and for legitimate business purposes.
*Agent signals:*
- Misuse of communication channels
- Inappropriate use of email or systems
- Attempts to hide or redirect communication

**R11 — Responsibility to Report Violations**
Employees must report concerns, violations, or unethical behavior to supervisors, managers, or legal counsel.
*Agent signals:*
- Discouraging reporting
- Hiding violations
- Avoiding escalation
- Suppressing compliance processes

**R12 — Appearance of Impropriety**
Employees must avoid behavior that appears unethical or improper, even if not explicitly illegal.
*Agent signals:*
- Unusual communication patterns
- Off-hours sensitive exchanges
- Unexpected cross-department coordination
- Suspicious behavior inconsistent with role

## Threat Evaluation Map
- R3, R5 -> Financial Fraud / Self-Dealing
- R6, R9 -> Policy Violation
- R11, R12 -> Strange Traffic / Governance Risk / Suppression
