# Corporate Rules and Code of Ethics

*Source: Corporate Code of Ethics, Department of Justice Archive*

## Core Principle
Employees must conduct business in accordance with all applicable laws and in a moral, honest, and ethical manner. Compliance is a condition of employment. Violations may result in disciplinary action, including termination.

## Rule Set (Content Analysis)

**R2 — Honest and Accurate Representation**
All business communications, records, and representations must be truthful and not misleading.
*Agent signals:*
- Misrepresentation of financial condition
- Pressure to alter or obscure facts
- Coordinated messaging that hides true business state
- Statements intended to mislead stakeholders

**R4 — No Bribery or Improper Payments**
No bribes, kickbacks, commissions, or improper payments may be given or received to gain advantage.
*Agent signals:*
- Requests or offers of side payments
- Exchange of benefits for favorable treatment
- "Off-the-record" financial arrangements

**R7 — Record Integrity and Ownership**
All records, documents, communications, and data belong to the company and must be preserved and handled properly.
*Agent signals:*
- Deletion or destruction of records
- Concealment of documents
- Avoiding documentation
- Moving discussions off-record

**R10 — Professional Conduct and Respect**
Employees must act with integrity, respect, fairness, and professionalism.
*Agent signals:*
- Abusive or coercive behavior
- Harassment or inappropriate communication
- Conduct inconsistent with company values

## Threat Evaluation Map
- R2, R4 -> Financial Fraud / Bribery
- R7 -> Data Destruction / Concealment
- R10 -> Misconduct
